Long before Tharsis, a prototype for closed-system community living was tested on Earth. It was not a lab. It was a five-story walk-up on 182nd Street, c. 1961. Its life-support systems were the public school (PS 32), the firehouse, the church, the park pool. Its success metric was not oxygen recycling, but social cohesion. Its lesson: Diversity isn't a challenge to manage; it's the feedstock for resilience. This page details how the cultural and architectural DNA of the Bronx is the unsung blueprint for Martian settlement.
